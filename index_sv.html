The Modern Football
Club Blueprint

A structured model to align sporting vision, scouting, talent development, performance, and decision-making in modern football environments.

The Problem

Why Most Clubs Struggle

Modern football organizations face structural inefficiencies that limit performance and long-term success.

🏚️

Siloed Departments

Scouting, performance, and finance operate independently with no shared data flow or common KPIs.

🔀

No Scouting Structure

Player identification varies by scout with no consistent methodology or profile alignment.

📊

Data Without Decisions

Clubs collect data but it rarely reaches decision-makers in a useful, actionable format.

⚡

Sporting Misalignment

Sporting vision, finance, and performance operate without shared objectives or timelines.

🌎

Regional Inconsistency

Talent identification varies across markets without a unified evaluation methodology.

Sports Department

Full Scouting Process

End-to-end workflow — from club methodology to final recommendation. 12 structured steps across 3 phases.

Phase 1 · What to Look For?
STEP 01

Club Methodology

Define game style: attacking phase, defensive phase, pressing, set pieces. Align with Technical Director on club identity.

STEP 02

Squad Succession Plan

Squad Ladder: contract expiries, age distribution, positional depth. Benchmark against champion squad profiles. Young 6%+.

STEP 03

Season Overview

End-of-season meeting: review collective + positional data. Output: positions needed and ideal attributes — no names yet.

STEP 04

Sporting Director Alignment

Seirullo model: define 8 player structures (Conditional, Cognitive, Emotional, Coordinative, Bioenergetic, Socioaffective).

Phase 2 · Analysis
STEP 05

Data Analysis

StatsBomb, Driblab, Impect, SkillCorner — per 90 stats, rankings by position, league comparison, age curve projection.

STEP 06

Video Scouting

Positional tendencies, pressing behavior, decision-making under pressure. Clips tagged to club methodology.

STEP 07

Live Scouting

In-person observation when data signals are strong. Focus: body language, leadership, positional intelligence off the ball.

STEP 08

Reference Category

AI similarity tools: who plays like Player X? Cross-league comparisons using Impect + StatsBomb models.

Phase 3 · Decision
STEP 09

Candidates Comparison

Side-by-side: league benchmarks, global benchmarks, scouted targets vs. current roster positional needs.

STEP 10

Game Intelligence

SkillCorner: runs (dropping off, coming short, pulling wide), pressing resistance, passing under pressure metrics.

STEP 11

Social & Family Context

References, pressure management, family support, city adaptation. Psychology assessment where available.

STEP 12

Financial Overview

Expected transfer fee, salary, agent commission, bonus structure. Salary budget category. ROI projection.

Coaching Management

Coaching Hire & Fire Framework

A structured process for evaluating coaching changes, running a rigorous hiring process, and benchmarking throughout a coach's tenure.

🔒 When to Fire — 4 Questions

QUESTION 1
Points vs Expected Points
Is underperformance structural or just variance? First 15 games = statistically significant. Firing with positive xG is a political, not sporting, decision.
QUESTION 2
Can He Rebound?
Research historical bad starts reversed. Simeone (Atletico 2012), Klopp (Dortmund 2014), Guardiola (City 2016) — all rebounded. Assess resilience first.
QUESTION 3
What is the Root Cause?
Classify before deciding: Tactical identity lost? Dressing room fractured? Injury crisis? External factors? Some problems are fixable — others are not.
QUESTION 4
Cost of Firing vs. Keeping?
Full financial analysis: remaining contract, transition cost, market availability of replacements. Document pre-agreed exit criteria before hiring.

🎯 8-Step Hiring Process

1
Define the profile needed — based on club identity, squad structure, budget
2
Build longlist of 5+ candidates using network + data tools
3
Score each candidate across 15+ criteria in 5 categories
4
Video analysis: tactical approach, pressing model, set pieces, player management
5
Reference checks: 3+ contacts from different clubs in his career
6
Interview + project presentation: how would he approach our club?
7
Present shortlist to President/CEO with full evidence package
8
Sign contract + define pre-agreed KPIs, success criteria, and exit conditions

Leadership

Sporting Director Evaluation Framework

8 key parameters scored 1–5. Based on the Mazatlan FC Due Diligence methodology. The SD is the spine of the entire sporting operation.

1

Identity & Squad Design

Define team identity, sporting objectives, player profile, game model and full squad design.

2

Match Control

Pre and post-match control of all teams. Know what is working and what is not at every level.

3

Coaching Calendar

Manage the planning calendar of all coaching staff across all teams and competitions.

4

Training Presence

Present at the majority of first team training sessions. Not optional — presence builds authority.

5

Medical Oversight

Monitor physical and medical reports. Set return dates for injured players. Own injury data.

6

Leadership & Discipline

Be the authority. Constant communication with leaders. Enforce discipline. The culture is set here.

7

Motivation & Empowerment

Motivate the work team. Empower department heads. Set measurable, clear goals for each area.

8

Reporting & Accountability

Monthly delivery from each department. Semester reports to President. Data drives every review.

Performance

Sport Science Department

Bridges data, physiology, and football performance. Supports coaching staff with objective metrics, player load management, and evidence-based protocols.

📡

Load Monitoring

GPS tracking, ATL/CTL ratios, HRV trends. Daily risk flags per player. Prevents overtraining and injury spikes.

🩺

Injury Prevention

Pattern identification from physical testing. Thermography CPK baseline. Return-to-play protocols with clear criteria.

🔄

Recovery Protocols

Structured recovery sessions between fixtures. Sleep, cold water immersion, and nutrition timed to match schedule.

🏋️

Physical Testing

Pre-season: strength, endurance, jump, speed, asymmetry. Anthropometric baseline (fat mass, muscle, hydration).

🥗

Nutrition & Hydration

Individual diet plans per player. Match-day nutrition protocol. Hydration tracking integrated with GPS load data.

😴

Sleep & Wellbeing

Sleep quality monitoring. Wellness questionnaires pre-training. Psychological readiness integrated with load decisions.

Staff Structure

Head of Sport Science

Leads the department. Designs annual monitoring plans, oversees testing batteries, communicates directly with Head Coach and SD.

Performance Analyst

Processes GPS and physical data. Builds load dashboards and flags injury-risk players daily using ATL/CTL and HRV trends.

Physiotherapist

Manages rehabilitation protocols. Works with performance staff to clear players from injury and design return-to-play criteria.

Nutritionist

Individual plans per player. Match-day nutrition protocols. Works with the hydration specialist on game-week structures.

S&C Coach

Strength and conditioning programs. Manages gym sessions, in-season maintenance loads, and pre-season physical block.

Psychologist

Mindset, pressure management, team cohesion, and individual player support. Integrated in the weekly staff meeting.

5-Step Valuation Process
STEP 01

Position & League Context

Define the exact role needed. Map league level, historical transfer data, and momentum via Impect.

STEP 02

Statistical Benchmarking

StatsBomb + Driblab per 90 metrics. Cross-league comparison. Age curve and trajectory analysis.

STEP 03

Market Context

Impect Squad Ratings. Supply and demand between selling and buying leagues. Classify by position scarcity.

STEP 04

Expected Performance

Predict output in target league. Adjust for historical adaptation patterns. Net Gains principle: future contribution, not past reputation.

STEP 05

Value Score Output

VS = Performance ÷ log(Salary). EPV blends predictive models + financial adjustments + contract length weight. Decision: Extend / Develop / Loan / Transfer.

Culture

How We Think & Work

The operating principles behind every decision in this blueprint. Culture is not a poster — it is behavior at every level.

🎯

Clarity Over Complexity

Simple, clear processes beat sophisticated systems nobody uses.

📊

Data Informs, Humans Decide

Data is context, not command. The SD and coach make the final call — with evidence.

🔄

Process Over Improvisation

A good process survives staff changes. Individual brilliance without process does not scale.

🤝

Alignment Before Action

Sporting, financial, and technical alignment before any signing, hire, or strategic decision.

🌱

Long-Term Thinking

Every decision evaluated at 1, 3, and 5 year horizon. Short-termism is the enemy of sustained performance.

⚡

Speed With Structure

Move fast, but within defined workflows. Urgency and discipline are not mutually exclusive.
